Governance by design

Artificial Imagination will be too powerful to govern after the fact.

For MEVENTA, governance is part of the intelligence architecture itself.

Architecture rule

Creativity, evaluation and governance remain separated.

Creativity proposes. Evaluation tests. Governance permits.

The more powerful Artificial Imagination becomes, the less acceptable it is to add governance afterwards.

EVIA separates candidate generation from acceptance, learning, output and action.

Separation

Candidate structures do not automatically become effective.

Not automatic memory

A candidate does not write itself into persistent memory.

Not automatic output

A candidate is not automatically communicated or published.

Not automatic learning

A candidate does not create learning without validation.

Not automatic action

A candidate triggers no action without mandate and governance.

Goal

Governance must be able to scale before autonomy scales.

This is how EVIA makes powerful imagination controllable by design.

Governance is not a brake on intelligence. It is the architecture that makes more powerful intelligence responsible enough to expand.
